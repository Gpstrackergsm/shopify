A Clinician's Guidebook for the NeuroAffective Relational Model (NARM)

Hi Reader,

We're thrilled to add The Practical Guide for Healing Developmental Trauma by Larry Heller & Brad Kammer to our library — the essential clinical guidebook for applying the NeuroAffective Relational Model (NARM).

NARM is a cutting-edge integrated mind-body framework that identifies and treats the complex ways childhood trauma manifests in interpersonal difficulties, maladaptive patterns, identity issues, and disrupted affect regulation.

This guidebook arms you with the skills to:

  • 🧠 Apply the four pillars of the NARM therapeutic model
  • 🌱 Address cultural and intergenerational trauma
  • 🔍 Differentiate shock trauma from complex developmental trauma
  • 📋 Work with Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) & C-PTSD
  • 💡 Integrate NARM into your existing therapeutic practice
  • 📖 Study annotated real-session transcripts from both authors

The Four Pillars of NARM

1. Therapeutic Contract — Grounding the work in the client's own intentions for healing

2. Deconstruction of Experience — Exploring how clients organize their adaptive survival patterns

3. Supporting Agency — Reinforcing authentic self-direction and self-esteem

4. Psychobiologic Shifts — Acknowledging somatic & relational shifts that signal real healing
